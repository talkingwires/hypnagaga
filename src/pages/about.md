Hypngaga is a vision quest. Something has gone terribly wrong on this planet of ours, and this author wants to know how and why. I am forty-something captive of a culture seemingly bent upon self-destruction. 

Yet, our culture tells us stories of a better world yet to spill into waking life. We show it in our movies, we write about it in our books, preach it in our pulpits, broadcast it to the world. Stories of a world long thought lost, but one that must surely up around the bend. 

These are the stories we experience on screens that are never far. *Nothing’s wrong*, the stories on these screens say. *All shall be well, and all mannar of things shall be well.* And so we march ever onwards, eyes on the horizon, that is what you are telling yourself when the ground pitches under your step. Something loose has given way under your foot, and the sensation jolts you awake.

Awake, and it’s still there. Something loose, maybe a pinecone underfoot, but then it does not matter because you have caught your balance. Your pace steadies, and you take a quick glance to your side. There I am next to you on this march. Fallen asleep on my feet too, from the looks of it.

You notice that glob of drool dangling from my chin. It grown impressively longer since you last noticed, and it wobbles in time with our steps. My head bounces in time, too, it reminds you of a baby in a carseat. I mouth, *ga ga ga ga ga ga ga*…
